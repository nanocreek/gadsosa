# Design Brief — Gad Sosa Personal Site

**Prepared for:** Designer / design partner
**Prepared by:** Gad Sosa
**Date:** July 2026

---

## 1. Who this is for

Gad Sosa is a technology executive with 20+ years leading software R&D, now focused on AI/ML — from model development through GPU-backed, production-grade systems — plus ML security and AI-driven engineering. This site is his professional home on the web.

**Primary goal:** win serious opportunities — full-time leadership roles (VP R&D / CTO) and long-term engagements (6+ months). Not short freelance gigs.

**Primary audience:** decision-makers who may hire him — founders, CEOs, boards, and executive recruiters. **Many are not deeply technical.** They buy trust and outcomes, not tech stacks. A secondary audience is the technical screener (a CTO or lead engineer) who checks credentials later in the process.

**Secondary goal:** discoverability. When someone searches his name or a topic he owns, he should show up, and show up looking credible.

---

## 2. What already exists

There is a working first version of the site (single-page, responsive). This brief is about elevating the **visual craft** to match the seniority of the audience — not redesigning from scratch unless a stronger direction emerges. The content, structure, and messaging are settled (see section 5).

---

## 3. The feeling we're going for

Senior, precise, quietly confident. This person runs R&D organizations and makes high-stakes calls — the design should feel like that: considered, uncluttered, trustworthy. Think "boardroom, not bootcamp."

**Avoid:** trendy startup clichés, heavy gradients, playful illustration, anything that reads as junior or template-y. No stock "team collaborating at laptops" photography.

**Lean into:** generous whitespace, a confident type hierarchy, restraint. One or two memorable details rather than many decorative ones.

---

## 4. Current visual system (a starting point, not a constraint)

- **Palette:** deep slate-navy (#0e1826), cool near-white (#f7f8fa), an engineered blue accent (#1f6feb / deep #0b3d91), and a restrained warm gold (#c8a24a) used sparingly for detail.
- **Type:** Fraunces (serif display) for headlines, Inter (sans) for body, IBM Plex Mono for labels/eyebrows.
- **Layout:** single page, sticky nav, numbered sections (01–07), a mix of light and dark sections for rhythm.

You are welcome to refine or replace any of this if you can justify a stronger direction for the brief. If you keep it, push the craft: spacing, type scale, and detail are where this either reads "executive" or "fine."

---

## 5. Sections (content is set)

1. **Hero** — headline about building trusted products and lean R&D teams; short positioning line; two CTAs; a row of past companies (DeepKeep, Informatica, ActiveTrail, MSN Israel, Brio/Dell).
2. **How I work** — four cards: R&D/CTO leadership, AI & ML (development to production, incl. GPU), R&D turnaround & scaling, AI-driven engineering (more output per dollar).
3. **Why work with me** — narrative + three proof points (Dell X-Series switch, scaling ActiveTrail, AI/ML at DeepKeep).
4. **References** — two testimonial cards (currently placeholders for real quotes).
5. **Insights** — a short list of articles (a lightweight blog).
6. **Experience** — a clean timeline of roles.
7. **Technical depth** — skills grid (for the technical checker).
8. **Get in touch** — LinkedIn + X links, location, and a contact form. No email or phone on the page.

---

## 6. What I'd love your eye on

- **The hero.** It carries the first impression. It should land the positioning in under five seconds without shouting.
- **A "signature" element.** One distinctive, memorable device that's tasteful and specific to a technology leader — not decoration for its own sake.
- **The company logo row.** Right now it's set in type. If real logos are appropriate and available, advise on treatment (consistent scale, monochrome, etc.).
- **Testimonials.** These are the trust anchor for non-technical buyers — make them feel credible and prominent, not like an afterthought.
- **A headshot/portrait treatment.** There is no photo yet. Recommend whether one belongs here and, if so, the style (formal, environmental, monochrome) and where it sits.

---

## 7. Practical requirements

- Fully responsive, mobile-first quality — many recruiters browse on phones.
- Accessible: readable contrast, visible keyboard focus, respects reduced-motion.
- Fast and lightweight; no heavy frameworks or bloated assets.
- Design should be delivered in a way a developer can implement cleanly (see the developer brief) — ideally Figma with a defined type scale, spacing system, and color tokens.

---

## 8. Deliverables (proposed — adjust in your quote)

- Desktop + mobile designs for the full single page.
- A small design-token sheet (colors, type scale, spacing).
- Any custom assets (icons, the signature element, logo-row treatment).
- Source file (Figma) handed off with the developer.

---
